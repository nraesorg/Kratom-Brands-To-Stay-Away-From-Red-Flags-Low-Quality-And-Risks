## Frequently Asked Questions

### What are the biggest red flags when choosing kratom vendors?

The most significant warning signs include lack of third-party lab testing, unrealistic health claims, extremely low prices, poor customer service, and no verifiable business information. Vendors who cannot provide current certificates of analysis or make illegal therapeutic claims should be avoided completely.

### How can I verify if a kratom vendor's lab reports are legitimate?

Contact the testing laboratory directly to verify the authenticity of lab reports. Legitimate labs will confirm whether they conducted the testing and issued the specific certificate. Also, check that the lab is accredited and specializes in botanical testing with proper equipment for kratom analysis.

### Are gas station kratom products always dangerous?

While not all gas station kratom is necessarily dangerous, these products typically lack proper quality control, testing, and storage protocols. The risk of contamination, adulteration, or poor quality is significantly higher compared to specialized kratom vendors who focus exclusively on kratom products.

### What should I do if I purchased kratom from a questionable vendor?

Stop using the product immediately if you experience any adverse effects. Contact the vendor for a refund if possible, and report any serious health issues to healthcare providers. Consider reporting problematic vendors to the American Kratom Association and relevant consumer protection agencies.

### How often should reputable kratom vendors test their products?

Quality vendors should test every batch of kratom they receive from suppliers. This means lab reports should be recent and specific to the actual product being sold, not generic reports from months or years ago. Batch-specific testing is the gold standard for kratom quality assurance.

### Can kratom vendors legally make health claims about their products?

No, kratom vendors cannot legally make therapeutic or medical claims about their products. The FDA has not approved kratom for any medical use, and vendors making such claims are violating federal regulations. Legitimate vendors focus on product quality and allow consumers to make their own informed decisions.

### What's the difference between expensive and cheap kratom in terms of quality?

While price doesn't always indicate quality, extremely cheap kratom often reflects poor sourcing, lack of testing, or inferior processing methods. Quality kratom requires investment in proper harvesting, processing, testing, and storage. However, excessively high prices don't guarantee quality either, so focus on vendor transparency and testing rather than price alone.
